🕵️ Phishing

Phishing is a type of social engineering where an attacker sends a fraudulent message designed to trick a person into revealing sensitive information or to deploy malicious software on the victim's infrastructure.

How to Spot it:

  • Urgency: Claims like "Account suspended" or "Immediate action required."
  • Fake Domains: `amaz0n.com` instead of `amazon.com`.
  • Generic Greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your name.

📱 Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Smishing is phishing conducted via Short Message Service (SMS), also known as text messaging.

Common Tactics:

  • Fake delivery notifications.
  • Bank security alerts.
  • "Win a Prize" notifications.

🔳 Quishing (QR Phishing)

Quishing involves malicious QR codes. Attackers place them in public areas (like parking meters or cafes) to redirect users to phishing sites or download malware.

Defense:

  • Never scan QR codes from untrusted sources.
  • Check if a QR code sticker has been placed over a legitimate one.
  • Use a scanner app that previews the URL before opening it.

🧩 Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files. The attacker then demands a ransom from the victim to restore access to the data upon payment.

Impact:

Ransomware can paralyze entire organizations, from hospitals to government agencies.

🎭 Social Engineering

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information.

It relies on human error rather than technical vulnerabilities. It's the "human hack."

🔐 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is a security system that requires more than one method of authentication from independent categories of credentials to verify the user's identity.

Layers:

  • Something you know (Password).
  • Something you have (Phone, Security Key).
  • Something you are (Biometrics).
