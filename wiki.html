🕵️ Phishing

Phishing is a type of Social EngineeringPsychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. where an attacker sends a fraudulent message designed to trick a person into revealing sensitive information.

📱 Smishing

Smishing (SMS PhishingPhishing attacks delivered via SMS/text messages instead of email.) is a cyberattack that uses deceptive text messages to trick victims into revealing sensitive information or clicking malicious links.

Common Tactics

  • Fake delivery alerts — "Your package is held. Verify here: [link]"
  • Bank fraud warnings — "Unusual activity detected. Confirm your details immediately."
  • Prize scams — "You've won! Claim your reward now."

How to Stay Safe

  • Never click links in unsolicited SMS messages.
  • Verify by calling the organization directly using an official number.
  • Report smishing attempts to your carrier (forward to 7726).

📷 Quishing

Quishing (QR Code PhishingPhishing attacks that embed malicious URLs inside QR codes to bypass email/link scanners.) uses malicious QR codes to redirect victims to fake websites designed to steal credentials or install malware.

Why It's Dangerous

  • QR codes are unreadable to the human eye — you can't spot a malicious URL before scanning.
  • Security tools that scan text-based links often cannot inspect QR code content.
  • Attackers place fake QR codes over legitimate ones in public spaces (parking meters, menus, posters).

How to Stay Safe

  • Use a QR scanner that previews the URL before opening it.
  • Inspect physical QR codes for stickers placed over the original.
  • Avoid scanning QR codes received in unexpected emails or messages.
